Professional

I'm a Management Information Systems student at the Smeal College of Business at Penn State University, graduating in May 2026 with a 3.95 GPA. My coursework has largely revolved around business strategy, data analytics, and systems design, adequately preparing me to optimize and leverage technology for businesses.

My experience spans interning at VentureDive in Karachi, where I built end-to-end data pipelines and learnt as much as I could about data engineering amongst experienced professionals, to client-facing technology work at ValueLink, where I currently serve as the primary point of contact for enterprise clients adopting complex appraisal management software. Earlier roles in product evaluation and IT coordination have given me a well-rounded view of how products are built and delivered.

I feel ready and excited to start this new chapter in my professional career as a Technology Consultant in New York next fall.

Personal

Outside of work and academics, I've been a fan of soccer and have supported FC Barcelona for as long as I can remember. I also took up weightlifting a little over two years ago and it has since become a passion of mine. Beyond that, I have a strong interest in cars and motorbikes and enjoy keeping up with the latest in both. More recently, I have also developed a habit of staying informed on current events, especially in areas like business, technology, and geopolitics, because I enjoy understanding what is happening in the world and how different industries and global trends connect.

I was raised in Pakistan before moving to the United States for university, and experiencing life in both countries has given me insight into two very different cultures and lifestyles. I’m incredibly grateful for that experience, as it has allowed me to develop a broader, more diverse perspective with which to approach any given situation. In addition, I’ve been fortunate to travel to many places in Europe and Asia, which has only deepened my appreciation for different people, cultures, and ways of thinking.
